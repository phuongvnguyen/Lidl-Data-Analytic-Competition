The Lidl purchasing group wants to expand our candy offering. These are store brand candies that we sell along the brand offerings. The idea is to create a brand new product. The team is discussing various options at the moment. Some prefer cookie-based sweets while others think that it should be gummies. The Divisional Director responsible for purchasing has decided to use a more data-driven approach. He contracted with a market research group to collect data on products in the market and their characteristics and customer sentiment. The market research data is now available and it is your job to

  1. find out which product characteristics drive customer sentiment and,

  2. subsequently make a recommendation on a new product.

$$\color{Green}{\underline{\textbf{Main Targets}}}$$

Thus, this project aims to generate ideas about a new candy product. To this end, we $\color{red}{\textbf{must completely understand the two following properties}}$ of our target customer

  1. Regarding the habit and preference, we must answer the four following questions such as: (1) what kind of candies they like most: candy with the ingredient of chocolate, peanut, or others? (2) How much amount of sugar they like most? (3) Which candies our customers prefer: hard or non-hard one? (4) Do our customer like one of many candies in a bag/ box?
  1. Related to income of customer, what income class our customers belong to? In particular, how the price impact the decision of our customer buy candy?

Thus, the solutions for the two above issues are very important since they enable us to create a new candy product which significantly attracts our cusmeter.

Based on the data on the candy market, one should think about the $\color{red}{\textbf{five following aspects}}$ when he/she plans to create a new candy product with a highly competitive win percentage.

  1. First, in terms of the ingredients, what are the ingredients of a new candy? For instance, among six ingredients such as chocolate, fruit, caramel, peanut, nougat, crisped rice, wafers, or a cookie component, and amount of sugar, how one can choose them to make a new candy brand with a high win percentage

  2. Second, regarding the type of candy: hard or not is better?

  3. Third, in regards to the shape of a candy: bar or others is better?

  4. Fourth, one of many candies in a bag/box or not.

  5. Last, related to the price of a new candy: cheap, affordable, relatively expensive, or expensive?

$$\color{green}{\underline{\textbf{Methodologies}}}$$

To this end, I use a wide range of state-of-the-art statistical and Machine Learning algorithms. Based on the dataset, in particular, I compute the Pearson correlations to find the linear relationship between two attributes. Especially, I focus on the linear relationships of eleven explanatory variables with the win percentage. Afterward, I use various tools of statistics and Machine Learning algorithms to evaluate the importance of each factor to the win percentages. For example, these tools include the F-value regression, Recursive Feature Elimination (RFE), Principal Component Analysis (PCA). More especially, two state-of-the-art ensemble methods such as Extra Tree and Gradient Boosting algorithms are used. The main purpose of this step is to have initial ideas of which inputs are most relevant to the win percentage. Based on the initial analysis, the next step is to train a Machine Learning model. However, I do not really know which Machine Learning algorithm is the most suitable method. Therefore, I train a number of Machine Learning models including both linear and non-linear algorithms. These competing ML models are then compared with each other by using the Mean Square Error (MSE) indicator. As a result, the Lasso algorithm is the best one because of the lowest MSE value.

To reveal the two above properties, I use the dataset and various tools of statistics and Machine Learning algorithms. Accordingly, I find the five following fundamental properties of the habit, preference, and income of our customers as the follows.

Our customers prefer the following ingredients most: chocolate, fruit, caramel, peanut, nougat, crisped rice, wafers, or a cookie component. They also prefer the sweet candies. On the other hand, our customers do not like the candies with the ingredients of fruit.

They typically prefer non-hard candy. Thus, it seems that our current and target customers are children or old people or maybe both.

Our custormer like the bar-shaped candy most.

They typically do not like one of many candies in a bag/ box.

They buy candy withour caring so much about price. Indeed, candies with high price typically do not significantly impact their dicision on buying or not?

Our customers typically do not care so much about the price of candy. For example, they buy candies with the high price.

def correlation_select(correlation, threshold):
    # picking up the upper triangle of the correlation matrix
    correlation_up=correlation.where(np.triu(np.ones(correlation.shape), k = 1).astype(np.bool))
    # selecting the attribute
    select_corr= [column for column in correlation_up.columns if any(abs(correlation_up[column])>threshold)]
    # printing
    print(Bold+ Red +'------------------------------------------------------------------'+End)
    print(Bold+ 'The number of the attributes has the higher-threshold correlation: {}'.
         format(len(select_corr)))
    print(Bold+ Red +'------------------------------------------------------------------'+End)
    print(Bold+'The list of the attributes has the higher-threshold correlation:' + End)
    print(select_corr)
    print(Bold+ Red +'------------------------------------------------------------------'+End)
    # recording the result
    ## creating a dataframe with three columns to record the result.
    record_select_correlation=pd.DataFrame(columns=['Attribute_1','Attribute_2','Correlation_Value'])
    ## doing record
    for column in select_corr:
        # Finding the first attributes wih the higher-threshold correlation
        Attribute_11=list(correlation_up.index[abs(correlation_up[column])>threshold])
        # Finding its counterpart attribute
        Attribute_21=[column for _ in range(len(Attribute_11))]
        # Fulfilling the correlation values

                                      'Attribute_2': Attribute_21,
                                      'Correlation_Value': Correlation_Value1})
        # Adding temp_df to the created dataframe
        record_select_correlation=record_select_correlation.append(temp_df_corr,ignore_index=True)
    print(Bold+ Red +'----------------------------------------------'+End)
    print(Bold+'Recording the higher-threshold correlations:')
    print(Bold+ Red +'----------------------------------------------'+End)

Training and Evaluating Models¶

Model Comparison

The general procedure is as follows:

Shuffle the dataset randomly. Split the dataset into k groups For each unique group:

  1. Take the group as a hold out or test data set
  2. Take the remaining groups as a training data set
  3. Fit a model on the training set and evaluate it on the test set
  4. Retain the evaluation score and discard the model
  5. Summarize the skill of the model using the sample of model evaluation scores

https://machinelearningmastery.com/k-fold-cross-validation/

  2. Recall: can be defined as the ratio of the total number of correctly classified positive examples divide to the total number of positive examples. High Recall indicates the class is correctly recognized (small number of FN).

  3. Precision: To get the value of precision we divide the total number of correctly classified positive examples by the total number of predicted positive examples. High Precision indicates an example labeled as positive is indeed positive (small number of FP). Precision is given by the relation:

High recall, low precision:This means that most of the positive examples are correctly recognized (low FN) but there are a lot of false positives.

Low recall, high precision:This shows that we miss a lot of positive examples (high FN) but those we predict as positive are indeed positive (low FP).

  1. F-measure: Since we have two measures (Precision and Recall) it helps to have a measurement that represents both of them. We calculate an F-measure which uses Harmonic Mean in place of Arithmetic Mean as it punishes the extreme values more. The F-Measure will always be nearer to the smaller value of Precision or Recall.

The Lasso Tuning¶

A tuning parameter, controls the strength of the L1 penalty. is basically the amount of shrinkage:

When = 0, no parameters are eliminated. The estimate is equal to the one found with linear regression.

  1. As increases, more and more coefficients are set to zero and eliminated (theoretically, when = , all coefficients are eliminated).

  2. As increases, bias increases.

  3. As decreases, variance increases.

Choosing Ensemble Methods¶

Ensemble methods combine several decision trees classifiers to produce better predictive performance than a single decision tree classifier. The main principle behind the ensemble model is that a group of weak learners come together to form a strong learner, thus increasing the accuracy of the model.

Bagging is used when the goal is to reduce the variance of a decision tree classifier, for instance.

Boosting is to improve the accuracy from the prior tree. Also, it isused to create a collection of predictors. In this technique, learners are learned sequentially with early learners fitting simple models to the data and then analysing data for errors. Consecutive trees (random sample) are fit and at every step, the goal is to improve the accuracy from the prior tree.

AdaBoost Regressor Tuning¶

The important parameters to vary in an AdaBoost regressor are learning_rate and loss. As with the previous algorithms, we will perform a randomized parameter search to find the best scores that the algorithm can do.

  1. n_estimatorsinteger, optional (default=50) The maximum number of estimators at which boosting is terminated. In case of perfect fit, the learning procedure is stopped early.

  2. learning_ratefloat, optional (default=1.) Learning rate shrinks the contribution of each regressor by learning_rate. There is a trade-off between learning_rate and n_estimators.

  3. loss{linear, square, exponential}, optional (default=linear) The loss function to use when updating the weights after each boosting iteration.
